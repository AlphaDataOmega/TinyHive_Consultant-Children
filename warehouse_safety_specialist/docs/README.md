# Warehouse Safety Specialist - Documentation

## Overview

This directory contains reference documentation for the warehouse_safety_specialist consultant agent, specializing in warehouse and retail safety, ergonomics, material handling, forklift operations, and OSHA compliance.

## Core Knowledge Areas

### 1. Safety and Health Program Management
- Management commitment and resource allocation
- Employee involvement and participation methods
- Injury reporting and early intervention protocols
- Distinguishing conditioning soreness from MSDs

### 2. Ergonomics and MSD Prevention
- Understanding musculoskeletal disorders (back injuries, carpal tunnel, tendinitis, rotator cuff injuries)
- Work-relatedness determination factors
- Benefits of ergonomic programs (reduced injuries, lower costs, improved morale)

### 3. Risk Factor Identification
- **Force**: Heavy lifting, pushing, pulling, pinch grip requirements
- **Repetition**: Continuous motions without adequate rest
- **Awkward/Static Postures**: Overhead reaching, kneeling, twisting, bent wrists
- **Contact Stress**: Hard/sharp edges, using hands as hammers

### 4. Safe Lifting and Material Handling
- Power grip (preferred) vs. pinch grasp (limited use)
- Proper lifting techniques (before, during, setting down)
- Best and preferred work zones
- Recommended working postures for head/neck, shoulders/arms, back, hands/wrists, feet/legs

### 5. Warehouse Operations Safety
- Stockroom and receiving areas (pallet handling, conveyor systems, floor conditions)
- Storage and shelving (organization, accessing high/low shelves)
- Cart and equipment maintenance
- Packaging and container standards
- Cold storage operations

### 6. Retail Store Safety by Department
- Front End (checkstand design, scanning, conveyor setup, cashier comfort, bagging, carryout)
- Stocking Department
- Bakery Department
- Meat and Deli Department
- Produce Department

### 7. Equipment Safety
- Powered Industrial Trucks (Forklifts): Operator requirements, pre-operation inspection, safe operation
- Pallet Jacks (Manual and Powered)
- Ladders and Step Stools
- Dock Equipment (loading docks, dock plates)

### 8. Emergency Response
- Injury response procedures and OSHA reporting requirements
- Fire safety (prevention and response)
- Chemical spill response
- Medical emergencies

### 9. Training Requirements
- Employee training components (MSD awareness, tools, practices, reporting)
- Management and supervisor training
- Training methods and sources
- Documentation requirements
- Language and accessibility considerations

### 10. Program Evaluation
- Evaluation methods (OSHA 300/301 records, workers' comp, interviews, observations)
- Leading indicators (hazards corrected, training completion, near-miss reports)
- Lagging indicators (injury rates, workers' comp costs, lost workdays)
- Continuous improvement and documentation

## Quick Reference - Storewide Ergonomic Solutions

| Task | Solution |
|------|----------|
| Heavy lifting | Use mechanical aids; get help for heavy/awkward items |
| Overhead reaching | Use step stools; rearrange storage |
| Low-level work | Use knee pads, kneelers, or stools |
| Prolonged standing | Provide anti-fatigue mats and footrests |
| Repetitive scanning | Use keyboards for quantities; maintain scanners |
| Pushing carts | Maintain wheels; push rather than pull |
| Cold storage | Use thermal gloves; rotate workers |
| Sharp edges | Round off or pad contact surfaces |
