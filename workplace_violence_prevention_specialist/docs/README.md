# Workplace Violence Prevention Specialist - Documentation

## Overview

This directory contains reference materials and standard operating procedures for workplace violence prevention in retail and late-night establishments.

## Core Knowledge Areas

### Violence Prevention Program Elements

The specialist applies four core program components:

1. **Management Commitment and Worker Involvement** — Ensuring leadership demonstrates concern for worker safety with equal priority to customer service, while engaging frontline workers in procedure development
2. **Worksite Analysis** — Conducting records reviews, screening surveys, and security analyses to identify risk factors
3. **Hazard Prevention and Control** — Implementing engineering, administrative, and work practice controls
4. **Safety and Health Training** — Providing comprehensive training on risks, procedures, and incident response

### Risk Factor Categories

**Environmental Risk Factors:**
- Cash handling operations
- Solo work assignments
- Isolated worksites
- Late-night hours
- Alcohol sales
- Poor lighting conditions
- Obstructed visibility

**Operational Risk Factors:**
- Limited cash controls
- Insufficient staffing
- Inadequate training
- Poor security measures
- Unclear emergency procedures

### Control Hierarchy

| Control Type | Effectiveness | Description |
|--------------|---------------|-------------|
| Engineering Controls | Most Effective | Physical improvements independent of human behavior |
| Administrative Controls | Moderate | Policies and procedures requiring worker compliance |
| Work Practice Controls | Moderate | Training and behavior modification |

### Engineering Controls Reference

**Visibility and Surveillance:**
- Low window signage
- Low shelving for sightlines
- Visible cash registers
- Curved mirrors
- Adequate interior/exterior lighting
- Video surveillance/CCTV
- Door detectors
- Height markers on exit doors

**Security Systems:**
- Alarm systems
- Panic buttons
- Handheld alarms/noise devices
- Cellular phones
- Private channel radios

**Access Control:**
- Fencing and traffic flow structures
- Door entry (buzzer) systems
- Bullet-resistant enclosures with pass-through windows

**Cash Controls:**
- Drop safes
- Limited cash signage

### Training Requirements

**All Employees:**
- Risk overview and prevention policy
- Cash handling and operational procedures
- Security measure usage
- Behavior recognition and de-escalation
- Safety device locations and operation
- Incident reporting procedures
- Robbery and shoplifting response
- Emergency action procedures

**Supervisors/Managers (Additional):**
- High-risk situation recognition
- Assignment safety considerations
- Hazardous situation handling
- Traumatized worker support

**Security Personnel (Specialized):**
- Worksite-specific procedures
- Psychological components of aggression handling
- Hostile situation de-escalation

### Incident Response Protocol

1. Provide immediate first aid and emergency medical treatment
2. Arrange transportation to medical facility if needed
3. Report to police and authorities
4. Secure premises to safeguard evidence
5. Prepare incident report immediately
6. Inform management
7. Provide post-incident support (trauma counseling, EAP, peer support)

### Recordkeeping Requirements

- OSHA Form 300 (if applicable)
- Medical reports (confidential, locked storage)
- Incident reports for abuse, verbal attacks, aggressive behavior
- Safety meeting minutes with corrective actions
- Training records with attendee and trainer information

## Regulatory Framework

- OSHA General Duty Clause, Section 5(a)(1)
- OSHA Publication 3153-12R (Workplace Violence Prevention for Late-Night Retail)
- State-specific workplace violence standards
- OSHA Form 300 recordkeeping requirements
