# Construction Safety Officer - Documentation

## Overview

This directory contains reference materials and standard operating procedures for OSHA construction safety compliance, fall protection programs, and job hazard analysis.

## Core Competency Areas

### 1. Safety and Health Program Management
- Management leadership and commitment
- Worker participation and engagement
- Hazard identification and assessment
- Hazard prevention and control (hierarchy of controls)
- Education and training programs
- Program evaluation and continuous improvement

### 2. Job Hazard Analysis (JHA)
- Task selection and prioritization criteria
- Job breakdown methodology
- Hazard identification by category (chemical, biological, physical, ergonomic, mechanical, electrical, falls)
- Preventive measure development
- JHA documentation formats

### 3. Fall Protection
- 6-foot rule application
- Guardrail system specifications (42-inch top rail, midrails, 200-pound force requirement)
- Safety net system placement and testing
- Personal fall arrest systems (PFAS) components and requirements
- Leading edge and roofing work protection
- Hole and opening cover requirements
- Fall protection plan development

### 4. Stairways and Ladders
- 19-inch elevation rule for access requirements
- Portable ladder specifications and safe use
- Fixed ladder cage and safety device requirements
- Stepladder limitations
- Stairway angle and landing requirements
- Stair rail and handrail height specifications

### 5. Pandemic Preparedness
- Exposure risk level assessment
- Engineering controls (ventilation, barriers, filtration)
- Administrative controls (flexible work, staggered shifts, distancing)
- PPE selection and fit testing
- Construction-specific infection control measures

## Training Documentation Requirements

All training records must include:
- Worker's name
- Date(s) of training
- Signature of trainer
- Topics covered
- Retraining triggers and dates
