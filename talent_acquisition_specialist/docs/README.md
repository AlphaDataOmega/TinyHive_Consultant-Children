# Talent Acquisition Specialist Documentation

## Overview

This directory contains reference materials and standard operating procedures for talent acquisition and hiring processes.

## Core Competency Areas

### 1. Talent Acquisition Mission and Principles

**Mission**: Attract and hire results-driven talent to build high-performing distributed teams through exceptional partnership and candidate experiences.

**Guiding Principles**:
- **Experience** — Stakeholder experience is central to the talent acquisition craft
- **Inclusivity** — Design fair and equitable processes into all activities
- **Predictability** — Deliver the right team members, in the right roles, at the right time

**Core Values**:
- Transparency in processes, timelines, and expectations
- Collaboration across teams to find the best candidates
- Efficiency that respects candidates' and interviewers' time
- Results-focused evaluation on job-relevant qualifications
- Diversity in building teams

### 2. Recruitment Process

**Standard Interview Stages**:
1. Application Process
2. Screening Call (30-40 minutes)
3. Assessment (role-dependent)
4. Technical Interview (role-dependent)
5. Team Interviews
6. References
7. Offer & Background Screening

**Timeline Expectations**:
| Stage | Expected Duration |
|-------|------------------|
| Application Review | 5 business days |
| Screening Call | 30-40 minutes |
| Technical Interview | 60-90 minutes |
| Team Interviews | 1-2 weeks |
| Reference Checks | 3-5 business days |
| Offer Process | 2-3 business days |

### 3. Job Descriptions

**Required Components**:
- Title and Overview
- Responsibilities (bulleted list)
- Requirements (skills, experience, qualifications)
- Job Grade/Level
- Performance Indicators (3-5 KPIs)
- Career Ladder
- Hiring Process

**Best Practices**:
- Use clear, action-oriented language
- Focus on role accomplishments
- Use inclusive language (avoid gendered terms)
- Avoid unnecessary years of experience requirements

### 4. Screening and Interviewing

**Screening Call Structure**:
- Learn about candidate experience and expectations
- Assess alignment with role requirements
- Discuss hiring eligibility and availability
- Review compensation expectations

**Interview Best Practices**:
- Complete interviewer training before conducting interviews
- Review job description and candidate materials
- Allocate 80-85% of time to interviewer questions
- Use STAR method for behavioral questions
- Avoid prohibited topics (age, race, gender, religion, etc.)

### 5. Technical Interviews

**Standard Format** (90 minutes):
- Preparation phase: candidate reviews materials 72+ hours before
- Interview phase: video call with screen sharing and live problem-solving
- Focus on collaboration and communication alongside technical skills

### 6. Panel Interviews

**Guidelines**:
- Conduct by video call
- Start with explanation and introductions
- No difficult or trick questions
- Focus on candidate, avoid interviewer-to-interviewer conversations
- Assign a lead interviewer to facilitate

### 7. Candidate Evaluation

**Scoring Definitions**:
| Score | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| Strong Yes | Exceeded expectations; confident candidate would succeed |
| Yes | Met expectations; qualified but may have outstanding questions |
| No | Did not meet expectations; has concerns but may have strengths |
| Strong No | Strongly did not meet expectations; significant concerns |

**Effective Feedback**:
- Include specific examples
- Align with competencies
- Relevant to the role
- Include both strengths and concerns

### 8. Internal Hiring

**Eligibility Requirements**:
- Performance: Performing or Exceeding level (Developing requires approval)
- Time in Role: Minimum 6 months (12 months for Sales Development)

**Process**:
1. Meet with current manager
2. Submit internal application
3. Proceed through standard interview process
4. Transition timeline: 4-6 weeks typical

### 9. Referral Program

**Definition of Referral**:
- Someone you have worked with or developed close relationship with
- You can speak to their skills and character
- You confirm they align with company values

**Not a Referral**:
- Acquaintances or friends of friends
- Candidates already sourced by recruiting
- Former employees
- Contract/temporary/internship roles

**Typical Bonus**: $5,000 (paid within 90 days of hire start date)

### 10. Reference Checks

**Requirements**:
- Collect 3 professional references
- At least 1 must be a past manager
- Completed before or concurrent with offer

### 11. Offer Process

**Offer Letter Components**:
- Position details (title, reporting, start date)
- Compensation (base salary, equity, vesting)
- Benefits overview
- Employment terms
- Required documents

**Equity Basics**:
- Stock options grant right to purchase at fixed price
- Typical vesting: 4-6 years with 1-year cliff
- Monthly vesting after cliff period

### 12. Privacy and Compliance

**Candidate Data Usage**:
- Communication about applications
- Administering application process
- Legal and regulatory compliance

**Candidate Rights**:
- Access personal data
- Correct inaccurate data
- Request deletion
- Withdraw consent
- Object to automated decisions

### 13. Security Awareness

**Interview Security Controls**:
- All interviews following recruiter screen should be on camera
- Compare video appearance to application photos
- Multiple interview stages with different interviewers

**Warning Signs**:
- Refusal to turn on camera
- Visual glitches suggesting deepfake
- Multiple voices or coaching
- Inconsistent responses
