# Benefits and Perks Specialist Documentation

## Overview

This directory contains reference materials and documentation for the Benefits and Perks Specialist consultant agent. The agent specializes in employee benefits administration, leave policies, insurance programs, and workplace perks.

## Core Knowledge Domains

### 1. Healthcare and Insurance

**Medical Insurance:**
- Plan options through benefits administrator
- Company contribution: 85% of employee premium, 50% of dependent premium
- Baseline plan with optional higher-cost alternatives
- Coverage begins first of month following hire date
- Medical waiver allowance ($25/month) for employees with alternative coverage

**Disability Insurance:**
- Short-term disability (STD) for all employees
- Long-term disability (LTD) for all employees
- Company pays 100% of premiums

**Life Insurance:**
- Death and dismemberment coverage at 1x earnings
- Age restrictions beginning at age 65
- Company-paid benefit

### 2. Paid Time Off

**Vacation Leave:**
- 15 days (3 weeks) per year
- Accrual rate: 1.25 days per month
- One week advance notice for scheduling
- Track in shared work calendar

**Sick Leave:**
- Accrual: 1 hour per 30 hours worked
- Maximum accumulation: 5 days
- Report to manager for payroll tracking

**PTO Accrual Rules:**
- PTO does not accrue during other paid leave
- Sabbatical time does not count toward sabbatical eligibility

### 4. Parental Leave

**New Parent Leave:**
- 12 weeks paid leave at 100% salary
- Available regardless of gender or sexual identity
- For birth or adoption
- Must be taken within one year

**State Benefit Coordination (California):**
- SDI and PFL provide partial wage replacement
- Company supplements to maintain full salary
- Cannot stack full company pay with state benefits

### 5. Sabbatical Program

**Eligibility Requirements:**
- 5 years continuous employment
- Fewer than 12 weeks PTO in preceding year
- 4 weeks advance notice

**Program Details:**
- 12 contiguous weeks (one quarter)
- Fully paid at 100% salary
- For passion projects, learning, volunteer work
- Presentation to team upon return expected
- Expectation of returning to work afterward

### 6. Protected Absences

**Pregnancy Disability Leave:**
- Up to 4 months for pregnancy-related disability
- Intermittent or reduced schedule available

**Bereavement Leave:**
- 3 consecutive paid days
- Covers spouse, domestic partner, children, parents, grandparents, siblings, in-laws

**Jury Duty/Witness Service:**
- Up to 3 paid days
- Remote work if service less than 4 hours/day
- Documentation required

**Other Statutory Leaves:**
- Granted as required by law
- Generally unpaid unless law requires otherwise

### 7. Enrollment Procedures

**New Hire Process:**
- Enroll before first of month following hire
- Review and compare plan options
- Select coverage levels
- Designate beneficiaries

**Qualifying Life Events:**
- Marriage or divorce
- Birth or adoption
- Loss of other coverage
- Spouse employment changes
- 30-day documentation window

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Baseline Plan | Company-designated medical plan with standard premium contribution |
| Medical Waiver | Opt-out of company medical coverage with monthly allowance |
| Qualifying Life Event | Life change permitting benefits changes outside open enrollment |
| Sabbatical | Extended paid leave for personal development after tenure milestone |
| Protected Absence | Leave required by law (bereavement, jury duty, pregnancy disability) |
| SDI | State Disability Insurance - California program for short-term disability |
| PFL | Paid Family Leave - California program for family care |

## Usage Guidelines

This agent should be consulted for:
- Benefits enrollment questions
- Leave policy clarification
- Insurance coverage explanations
- PTO accrual and tracking
- Sabbatical eligibility assessment
- Protected absence procedures
- Holiday schedule inquiries

- Benefits appeals and exceptions
