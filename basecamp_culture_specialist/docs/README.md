# Basecamp Culture Specialist Documentation

## Overview

This directory contains reference materials and documentation for the Basecamp Culture Specialist consultant agent. The agent specializes in implementing remote-first company cultures, asynchronous communication practices, work cycle methodologies, and employee policies based on the 37signals/Basecamp model.

## Core Knowledge Domains

### 1. Remote Work Philosophy

**Foundational Principles:**
- Fully distributed teams work effectively across any geography
- Location independence - employees choose where to live and work
- Remote work is the default, not an exception
- Asynchronous communication as primary mode of collaboration
- Trust employees to manage their own time and output

**Key Practices:**
- No location requirements for employment
- Tax and compliance considerations when moving across borders
- Personal device vs. work device boundaries
- Home office setup support and coworking stipends

### 2. Six-Week Work Cycles

**Cycle Structure:**
| Phase | Duration | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Work Cycle | 6 weeks | Active development and project execution |
| Cooldown | 2 weeks | Bug fixes, documentation, planning |

**Communication Cadence:**
| Mechanism | When | Required |
|-----------|------|----------|
| Daily Check-in | Afternoon | 2x/week minimum |
| Weekly Check-in | Monday morning | Weekly |
| Kickoffs | Second Friday of cooldown | Per team |
| Heartbeats | First Friday of cooldown | Per team |

**Shape Up Methodology:**
- Fixed time, variable scope approach
- Appetite-based project sizing
- Hill charts for progress tracking
- Betting table for cycle planning

### 3. Asynchronous Communication

**Core Principles:**
- Default to messages, todos, and documents over real-time communication
- Allow others to respond on their schedule
- Use real-time communication (calls, pings) only when async isn't efficient
- Radiate information about current work

**Information Radiating:**
- "What did you work on today?" - Daily context sharing
- "What will you be working on this week?" - Weekly visibility
- Kickoffs - Upcoming cycle summary
- Heartbeats - Completed work celebration

**When to Use Real-Time:**
- Tight collaboration requirements
- Emergency situations
- Complex discussions requiring rapid back-and-forth
- Relationship building and team bonding

### 4. Managers of One

**Self-Management Expectations:**
- Set your own direction when not explicitly given
- Determine what needs to be done and do it
- Spend time productively without oversight
- Identify initiatives and improvements proactively
- Ask for help when needed, but default to self-resolution

**Manager Responsibilities:**
- Provide clear expectations and context
- Give frequent, candid feedback
- Create opportunities for skill demonstration
- Conduct regular 1:1 meetings
- Document performance outcomes

### 5. Work-Life Balance

**Standard Policies:**
| Policy | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Standard Week | 40 hours |
| Summer Hours | 32 hours (4-day weeks), May 1 - August 31 |
| Off-Hours Work | Compensated with equivalent time off |
| Sabbatical | 6 weeks every 3 years |

**Philosophy:**
- Limiting hours forces prioritization of meaningful work
- Sleep and life outside work are not to be sacrificed
- Sustainable pace over heroic effort
- Quality over quantity of hours

### 6. Device Management

**Managed Devices (macOS):**
- Kandji for central management
- Standard configuration: encryption, firewall, password rules
- Automatic security updates
- Remote wipe capability

**Managed Devices (Linux):**
- Omarchy distribution (in-house)
- Built-in security configuration
- 1password for credential management
- Tailscale VPN for access control

**Unmanaged Devices:**
- iOS, Android, Windows devices
- Can access company apps (Basecamp, HEY)
- Cannot store code or secrets
- Cannot access sensitive systems

**Security Principles:**
- Work devices only for company code and secrets
- No personal data on work devices
- Company reserves right to seize devices if required

### 7. Moonlighting Guidelines

**Permitted:**
| Activity | Conditions |
|----------|------------|
| Friend side gigs | Occasional, not ongoing |
| Speaking engagements | Without excessive travel |
| Different side business | Few hours/week |
| Advisory/board roles | No conflict, under few hours/month |
| Volunteer work | Not full-time commitment |

**Prohibited:**
| Activity | Reason |
|----------|--------|
| Competing company work | Direct conflict |
| Industry consulting | Potential conflict |
| Heavy speaking circuit | Too disruptive |
| Aggressive self-promotion | Creates conflict |
| Demanding side projects | Impacts performance |

**Evaluation Questions:**
1. Is it competitive?
2. Does it take disproportionate time?
3. Does it require absence during work hours?
4. Does it use same skills company pays for?

### 8. Career Progression

**Level Structure:**
| Level | Title | Typical Tenure |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| L1 | Junior | 0-2 years |
| L2 | Mid-level | 2-4 years |
| L3 | Senior | 4+ years |
| L4 | Lead | Senior + leadership |
| L5 | Principal | Decade+ with exceptional impact |

**Progression Expectations:**
- Junior to Senior: 4 years (2 + 2)
- Mid-level to Senior: 2 years
- Senior to Lead/Principal: Not expected; most remain Senior
- Principal: Reserved for industry-impacting innovation

**Performance Review Cadence:**
| Year | Reviews |
|------|---------|
| First year | 3-month, 6-month, 12-month |
| Subsequent | Annual (anniversary date) |

### 9. Compensation Philosophy

**Principles:**
- Top 10% of industry at San Francisco rates
- Location-independent pay (same role/level = same pay)
- Salary floor for non-technical roles ($73,500)
- Annual market review with January adjustments
- No pay decreases even if market drops

**Financial Benefits:**
| Benefit | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Profit Sharing | 10% of profits distributed by tenure (2+ years) |
| Liquidity Pool | 10% of company value if sold (2+ years) |
| 401K Match | 100% up to 6% of salary |

### 10. Onboarding Structure

**Timeline:**
| Phase | Activities |
|-------|------------|
| Pre-start | Equipment order, system setup |
| Day 1 | Welcome project, manager meeting |
| Week 1 | Team introductions, buddy assignments |
| First 90 days | Progressive responsibility increase |
| 3 months | Expected to be fully integrated |

**Key Contacts:**
- Manager: Direction, expectations, performance
- Ops Buddy: Technical setup, accounts
- 37signals Buddy: General guidance, culture questions
- People Ops: Benefits, policies, compensation

### 11. Company Rituals

**Regular Rituals:**
| Ritual | Frequency | Purpose |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| All Hands | End of each cycle | Company-wide updates |
| Meet-ups | Twice yearly | In-person team building |
| Everyone on Support | Periodic | Customer empathy building |

**Meet-up Structure:**
- Duration: 1 week (Sunday arrival, Friday departure)
- Location: Different city each time
- Mix of scheduled talks and unstructured time
- Peer appreciation and lightning talks
- Strategy sessions and 1:1 meetings

## Usage Guidelines

When providing guidance:
1. Reference specific 37signals practices with context for adaptation
2. Consider organization size when recommending practices
3. Acknowledge practices may need modification for different industries
4. Direct legal questions to qualified employment attorneys
5. Emphasize cultural fit over rigid policy implementation
