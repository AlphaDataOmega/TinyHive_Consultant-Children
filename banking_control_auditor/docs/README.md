# Banking Control Auditor - Documentation

## Overview

This consultant child specializes in banking internal controls, OCC compliance, and financial institution audits based on the OCC Comptroller's Handbook standards.

## Core Knowledge Areas

### Internal Control Components

The five essential components of effective internal control systems:

1. **Control Environment** - Board and management commitment, organizational structure, ethics, personnel policies
2. **Risk Assessment** - Identification, measurement, and analysis of risks at all levels
3. **Control Activities** - Policies, procedures, and practices ensuring directive compliance
4. **Accounting, Information, and Communication Systems** - Transaction capture, reporting, and information flow
5. **Self-Assessment and Monitoring** - Ongoing oversight and independent review

### Regulatory Framework

| Regulation | Scope | Key Requirements |
|------------|-------|------------------|
| 12 CFR 30 | All insured national banks | Safety and soundness standards, internal control appropriate to size and activities |
| 12 CFR 363 | Banks with $500M+ assets | Annual independent audits, management assertions, IPA attestations |
| 15 USC 78m | Banks with registered securities | Internal accounting controls, transaction authorization, asset accountability |

### Internal Control Objectives

Effective internal control provides reasonable assurance that:

- Bank operations are efficient and effective
- Recorded transactions are accurate
- Financial reporting is reliable
- Risk management systems are effective
- The bank complies with laws, regulations, and policies

## Examination Procedures

### Planning Phase

1. Review previous examination reports and OCC databases
2. Review internal and external audit reports
3. Review management assertions and IPA attestations
4. Discuss with management on control changes and factors
5. Obtain list of outstanding control deficiencies
6. Determine reliance level on bank's assessments
7. Select appropriate procedures and ICQs

### Assessment Quality Scale

- **Strong** - Comprehensive, effective controls with minimal weaknesses
- **Satisfactory** - Adequate controls with manageable weaknesses
- **Weak** - Significant deficiencies requiring immediate attention

## Control Activities Checklist

### Sensitive Position Controls

- [ ] Segregation of duties (no single person originates, processes, and reconciles)
- [ ] Rotation of duties in sensitive positions
- [ ] Mandatory two-week consecutive absence requirement
- [ ] Dual control over cash, negotiable collateral, official checks
- [ ] Safeguards for wire transfer access
- [ ] Pre-employment background checks
- [ ] Periodic review of employee accounts

### Verification Processes

- [ ] Independent review of each function's performance
- [ ] Timely and independent account reconciliation
- [ ] Resolution and clearing of outstanding items
- [ ] Minimal policy overrides with exception reporting
